Overview

In this workshop, we will explore the future of regulatory submissions, focusing on three pivotal advancements: full reproducibility, open-source technologies, and interactive data capabilities. We‚Äôll examine how Continuous Integration and Continuous Deployment (CICD) and containerization are transforming submissions by enabling reproducible, automated processes. We‚Äôll then look at how open-source tools and R packages are reshaping clinical reporting, offering flexible, transparent solutions. Finally, we‚Äôll dive into interactive data tools like {teal} and Shiny, showcasing their role in dynamic data exploration and visualization.

Learning objectives

After a vision-filled motivational introduction, and through a succession of practical examples, this session will empower R developers, data scientists, and clinical researchers to adopt innovative, future-ready approaches.

Is this course for me?

Ever wondered how clinical trials reporting may look in the future? Are you interested in learning about some of the latest tools and technologies transforming regulatory submissions? Do you want to explore the potential of R in clinical research? If you answered yes to any of these questions, this workshop is for you!

The workshop is not designed only for those who are masters in R. We will bring you along an intuitive path to produce reproducible (and interactive) reports.

Instructors

Davide Garolini (he/his) is a Statistical Engineer with an M.Phys. in Biophysics and a Ph.D. in Computational Neuroscience, has over ten years of R programming and package development expertise, specializing in clinical data analysis and regulatory deliveries. He significantly contributes to the NEST project by improving key tools like {rtables}, {tern}, and {gtsummary} for regulatory compliance. Davide strongly advocates for open-source collaboration, promoting advancements in clinical research.



Leena Khatri (she/her) is a Senior Data Scientist in Product Development Data Sciences at Roche. She is currently the Product Owner of the NEST data sciences expert team and associated packages such as {rtables}, {tern}. In addition she leads statistical programming teams, supporting late phase programs in Immunology. Leena is a strong advocate for the end to end usage of R in clinical reporting.
